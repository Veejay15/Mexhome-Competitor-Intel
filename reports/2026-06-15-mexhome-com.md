## 5. Recommended Actions for MexHome

1. **Strengthen the Mexico City for-sale pages to hold new position-1 rankings.** The homepage picked up position 1 for "properties for sale in mexico city," "mexico city property for sale," and "mexico city properties for sale" this week, all at 590 monthly searches. MexHome has /area/mexico-city/, /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-condos-for-sale/, /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-houses-for-sale/, and /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-land-for-sale/. These rankings are landing on the homepage, not on the dedicated Mexico City pages. Internal linking from the homepage to the Mexico City section should be reinforced, and the Mexico City area pages should be expanded with fresh content (neighborhood guides, market data) to shift traffic to the correct destination pages and reduce ranking dependency on the homepage.

2. **Build on the San Miguel de Allende link momentum with stronger on-page content.** Three editorial domains (englishleaflet.com, erafame.com, biozhere.com) linked directly to /area/san-miguel-de-allende/ this week with contextually rich anchors including "San Miguel de Allende property," "premium real estate San Miguel de Allende," and "san miguel de allende property market." The existing page at /area/san-miguel-de-allende/ should be expanded with a current market overview, buyer FAQ section, and local data to match the authority signals these links are now conferring. The new /area/san-miguel-de-allende/apartments-for-rent-in-san-miguel-de-allende/ page should be internally linked from that hub.

3. **Optimize the beachfront and luxury rental pages for the newly gained high-opportunity terms.** "Mexico property for sale beachfront" and "beach property in mexico for sale" (each 320 searches/month) jumped from positions 8 to 10 all the way to position 1 this week, both landing on the homepage. MexHome has /area/mexico-vacation-rentals/mexico-beach-vacation-rentals/ and the new /area/mexico-vacation-rentals/mexico-luxury-vacation-rental/. A dedicated for-sale beachfront page (not a vacation rental page) does not exist in the current page list. Building /area/condos-for-sale/ and /area/homes-for-sale/ already exist as generic pages, but a focused /area/beachfront-property-for-sale/ or equivalent would anchor these rankings on a purpose-built page rather than the homepage, reducing volatility risk.

4. **Pursue more links from the editorial pattern established this week.** The best new links came from buyer-guide content on mid-to-high authority domains covering topics such as "how to vet a Mexican real estate developer," "mistakes foreign buyers make," and "how to buy remotely." MexHome already has relevant blog content on these topics (e.g., /blog/risks-and-rewards-of-pre-construction-real-estate-in-mexico/, /blog/questions-foreigners-ask-buying-mexico-real-estate/). Proactive outreach to authors producing this type of content and ensuring MexHome's most link-worthy guides are easy to cite (with clear statistics, structured headers, and embeddable data) will compound the link velocity seen this week.

5. **Clean up lost-link destinations pointing to deleted page URLs.** Several lost backlinks this week pointed to URLs that no longer exist on mexhome.com, specifically paginated archive URLs like /blog/properties-in/nuevo-vallarta-west/page/26/ and /blog/properties-in/nuevo-vallarta-west/page/28/, plus the old /area/jalisco-real-estate/puerto-vallarta-real-estate/ path. These 404 and crawl-error losses represent link equity evaporating. Implementing 301 redirects from those defunct paths to the closest current equivalent pages (e.g., /area/riviera-nayarit-real-estate/nuevo-vallarta/ and /area/puerto-vallarta-real-estate/) would recover whatever crawlable link value remains from those referring pages.
