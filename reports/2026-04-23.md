## 5. Recommended Actions

1. **Build or optimize a page targeting "puerto vallarta homes for sale" immediately.** Mexico Life just fell out of the top 10 for this 2,900-monthly-search transactional keyword. Keyword difficulty is 20. If MexHome has an existing Puerto Vallarta listings or real estate page, update the title tag, H1, and introductory content to target this exact phrase. Add internal links from blog posts and neighborhood pages pointing to it.

2. **Create a "homes for sale in Puerto Vallarta, Jalisco" page or section.** Mexico Life dropped from position 5 to unranked across multiple ranking types for this modifier. The Jalisco qualifier (1,300 searches/month) is used by searchers with clear buying intent. A dedicated page or a well-optimized section on the main PV page targeting this phrase can capture the vacancy.

3. **Audit and build destination pages for Bucerias, Chacala, San Pancho, and Nuevo Vallarta.** Mexico Life is gaining SERP feature placements for destination queries in these markets while losing transactional ones. If MexHome does not have standalone pages for each of these locations, build them now. Prioritize Nuevo Vallarta (12,100 monthly searches for the head term) and Bucerias (2,900 monthly searches).

4. **Pursue an AMPI membership or partner listing on vallartanayaritmls.com.** This is Mexico Life's strongest backlink in the visible export. A dofollow link from the official AMPI Vallarta and Compostela MLS site carries genuine topical authority. MexHome should contact AMPI Vallarta directly to explore whether a listing, membership badge, or partner link is available.

5. **Develop one retirement-focused editorial asset and pitch it to publications like Money Digest.** Mexico Life earned a dofollow link from a retirement beach towns article. This link pattern is straightforward to replicate. MexHome should produce a data-backed or list-based piece on retiring in Mexico, Puerto Vallarta, or specific beach towns, and pitch it to personal finance and lifestyle publications that cover expat and retirement topics.

6. **Review MexHome's internal linking structure for Puerto Vallarta neighborhood pages.** Mexico Life's Old Town page is ranking position 6 for the broad "puerto vallarta real estate" term (3,600 searches/month). This suggests their neighborhood pages are driving authority to their main market pages. MexHome should ensure that every neighborhood and development page links back to its primary city-level Puerto Vallarta page with keyword-relevant anchor text.

7. **Do not attempt to replicate Mexico Life's blogspot backlinks.** The majority of their top-row backlink export consists of nofollow blogspot spam with thousands of outbound links. These are worthless for SEO and may create a toxic link profile. MexHome's link-building efforts should focus entirely on the editorial and association-based approaches described above.
