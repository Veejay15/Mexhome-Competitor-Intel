## 5. Recommended Actions for MexHome

1. **Strengthen the beachfront property landing page or create a dedicated hub.** The homepage is now ranking position 1 for "mexico property for sale beachfront" (320 volume) and "beach property in mexico for sale" (320 volume), but the homepage is a poor long-term holder for these queries. MexHome does not have a dedicated beachfront-specific search or editorial page in the existing page list beyond /area/mexico-vacation-rentals/mexico-beach-vacation-rentals/. A purpose-built page targeting beachfront-for-sale intent would hold these rankings more securely and improve conversion.

2. **Build on the Naya Homes partnership for dofollow link acquisition.** The press release syndication this week produced links almost exclusively nofollow. MexHome should negotiate with Naya Homes for dofollow editorial placements, such as a dedicated property or guide page co-authored with Naya Homes, ideally pointing to high-authority area pages (Los Cabos, Riviera Nayarit) rather than the homepage alone.

3. **Recover the lost middleclasshomes.net link.** The removal of a DR 65 followed link from a relocation-guide article (anchor: "cities of Cabo San Lucas") targeting the Los Cabos real estate page is the only quality loss this cycle. MexHome should reach out to the publisher to offer updated, accurate content about Cabo San Lucas as a relocation destination, with a request to restore the link.

4. **Expand Mexico City content depth.** Three Mexico City property queries ("mexico city properties for sale," "mexico city property for sale," "properties for sale in mexico city," all 590 volume) jumped from positions 2-4 to position 1 this week, indicating rising algorithmic confidence in the existing /area/mexico-city/ page cluster. MexHome already has /area/mexico-city/, /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-condos-for-sale/, /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-houses-for-sale/, and /area/mexico-city/mexico-city-land-for-sale/. The recommended action is to expand the depth of each of those existing pages with current inventory counts, neighborhood breakdowns, and lifestyle content to consolidate and defend the position-1 foothold before competitors respond.

5. **Defend "homes in mexico" (3,600 volume) at position 1.** This is the only notable decline visible in the data, slipping from 1 to 2. Given the homepage holds multiple high-volume position-1 rankings, a minor on-page refresh (updated schema markup, stronger above-the-fold signals for the "homes in mexico" intent) should be evaluated to reclaim top position before it drifts further.

6. **Capitalize on the San Pancho ranking momentum with content depth.** "San pancho nayarit real estate" moved from position 2 to position 1 (590 volume) on /area/riviera-nayarit-real-estate/san-francisco-mexico/. MexHome already has this page plus /area/san-pancho-vacation-rentals/ and /area/san-pancho-vacation-rentals/san-pancho-apartments-for-rent/. Adding current listing counts, a neighborhood overview section, and internal links from the San Pancho blog content (/blog/real-estate-san-pancho-mexico/, /blog/discover-san-pancho-a-hidden-gem-on-mexicos-riviera-nayarit-coast/) to the area page would reinforce this position and reduce rebound risk.
