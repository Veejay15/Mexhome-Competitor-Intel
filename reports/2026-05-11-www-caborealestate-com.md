## 5. Recommended Actions for MexHome

Cross-reference against mexhomeExistingPages confirms MexHome has strong Los Cabos area coverage (including `/area/los-cabos/`, `/area/los-cabos/cabo-san-lucas/`, `/area/los-cabos/san-jose-del-cabo/`, and several sub-pages) and a deep blog library. The following actions are grounded in what Cabo Real Estate built this week and where MexHome's existing content does not yet match those specific angles.

1. **Build a beachfront homes pricing guide for Los Cabos.** A search of mexhomeExistingPages finds no page targeting "beachfront homes Los Cabos pricing" or a comparable buyer-education piece on cost and ownership realities of Los Cabos beachfront property. Cabo Real Estate is now occupying this research query. MexHome should publish a blog post or guide that covers current price ranges by zone (Corridor, Cabo San Lucas beach, East Cape), what drives beachfront premiums, and what buyers need to know about fideicomiso and coastal zone restrictions. This directly counters the competitor's new post.

2. **Publish a Pacific side vs. Sea of Cortez side comparison guide for Los Cabos.** MexHome has individual area pages for Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo but no content addressing the buyer question of which side of the peninsula is the better fit. This is a high-value, low-competition informational format that ranks well for undecided buyers. The piece should address climate differences, beach conditions, lifestyle character, typical buyer profiles, and price differentials, and link out to MexHome's existing area and listing pages on both sides.

3. **Audit and refresh existing Los Cabos blog content for topical authority.** MexHome has multiple Cabo-related posts (including `/blog/best-places-to-buy-real-estate-in-the-los-cabos-area/`, `/blog/explore-cabo-san-lucas-real-estate/`, `/blog/buying-real-estate-in-cabo/`, and others). Given Cabo Real Estate is actively publishing new content in this space and touching its core area pages simultaneously, MexHome should review whether its existing Cabo posts are current on pricing, reference recent developments, and include internal links to area and development pages. Stale content in a contested market loses rankings to freshly updated competitors.

4. **Consider a "which area of Los Cabos is right for me?" interactive or structured guide.** The two posts Cabo Real Estate published this week both serve the buyer-orientation phase. MexHome's existing Los Cabos content is largely area-descriptive rather than decision-facilitating. A structured comparison guide (or even a well-organized blog post with a comparison table) that helps buyers self-select between Cabo San Lucas, San Jose del Cabo, the Corridor, Todos Santos, and East Cape would strengthen MexHome's position at the top of the funnel for this market and link naturally to every existing Los Cabos sub-area page already in the site.
