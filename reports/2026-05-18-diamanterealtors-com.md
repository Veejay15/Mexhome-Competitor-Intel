Diamante's move is specific: they are building a recurring, branded quarterly market report series for the BCS MLS. The closest existing content is blog posts covering Los Cabos real estate topics generally (e.g., `/blog/cabo-real-estate-opportunities-and-trends/`, `/blog/unlocking-opportunities-los-cabos-real-estate-investments/`), but none match the structured, data-anchored, quarter-specific report format Diamante is building.

1. **Launch a quarterly BCS/Los Cabos market report series.** Diamante is building SEO equity around "[location] MLS real estate market report [quarter] [year]" queries. MexHome should publish its own equivalent, drawing on available transaction data, median price trends, and inventory levels for Los Cabos and Baja California Sur. Publish it under a consistent slug structure such as `/blog/los-cabos-real-estate-market-report-q1-2026/` and commit to quarterly cadence. Over time this compounds into a rankable archive.

2. **Extend the same report format to Riviera Nayarit and Puerto Vallarta.** Diamante is focused on BCS. MexHome's primary geographic strength is Puerto Vallarta and Riviera Nayarit. Quarterly market reports for those markets, where MexHome has deeper agent presence and more existing content infrastructure, would be harder for Diamante to replicate and could capture high-intent informational traffic in MexHome's core territory.

3. **Strengthen the existing Los Cabos area pages with market data context.** Pages like `/area/los-cabos/`, `/area/los-cabos/cabo-san-lucas/`, and `/area/los-cabos/san-jose-del-cabo/` already exist. Adding current market data callouts, even brief summary statistics updated quarterly, would improve the relevance signal on those pages without requiring a separate report build.
