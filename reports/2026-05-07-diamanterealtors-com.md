## 5. Recommended Actions for MexHome

The new pages this week are agent profiles and do not require a content response. However, the broader pattern visible in Diamante's sitemap reveals meaningful content gaps in MexHome's Baja coverage. The following actions are specific and prioritized for this week.

**1. Audit and strengthen the existing `/area/los-cabos/` hub and its child pages.**
MexHome already has pages for Cabo San Lucas, San Jose del Cabo, Todos Santos, La Paz, East Cape, Loreto, and Los Cerritos (confirmed in `mexhomeExistingPages`). Diamante has built dozens of sub-neighborhood pages under each of these locations (Palmilla, Chileno Bay, Pedregal, Querencia, El Tezal, La Paz Downtown, La Paz Beaches, Fidepaz, Esterito, and many more). Pull traffic data on MexHome's existing Baja area pages. Identify which pages are thin or have low engagement and prioritize content expansion on those first, starting with the highest-traffic locations.

**2. Evaluate building a Loreto sub-area content cluster.**
Diamante has built pages for Loreto Centro, Loreto Miramar, Loreto South, Loreto Zaragoza, Loreto Ligui, Loreto San Cosme, Loreto Infonavit, Danzante Bay, Nopolo, and Puerto Escondido (Loreto area). MexHome has `/area/los-cabos/loreto/` and `/area/los-cabos/loreto/danzante-bay/` and `/area/los-cabos/loreto/nopolo/`. The parent Loreto page exists but the sub-neighborhood depth is thin compared to Diamante. Consider adding content depth to the existing Loreto page rather than building new pages, and assess whether Danzante Bay and Nopolo pages need content expansion.

**3. Evaluate building an East Cape sub-area content cluster.**
MexHome has pages for Cabo Pulmo, El Cardonal, La Ribera, Los Barriles, Miraflores, Santiago, and Zacatitos under East Cape (confirmed in `mexhomeExistingPages`). Diamante has built additional sub-pages for Los Barriles Centro, Buena Vista, Rancho Leonero, La Ribera Centro, Zacatitos, San Bartolo, Santa Maria, and several ranch-area communities. Review MexHome's existing East Cape child pages for thin content and prioritize expanding the Los Barriles and Buena Vista pages specifically, as those are active buyer markets.

**4. Evaluate building a La Paz sub-neighborhood content cluster.**
MexHome has `/area/los-cabos/la-paz/` plus five child pages (Bay of Dreams, El Sargento, El Tecolote, La Ventana, San Juan de la Costa, and property-type pages). Diamante has built La Paz Downtown, La Paz Beaches, Fidepaz, Esterito, El Mogote, El Centenario, Colina del Sol, Chametla, Bellavista, El Comitan, and more than a dozen additional La Paz neighborhood pages. This is the deepest gap. Determine if La Paz search volume justifies building additional neighborhood pages under the existing `/area/los-cabos/la-paz/` hub. If so, La Paz Downtown and La Paz Beaches are the highest-intent targets to build first.

**5. Do not build a new agent directory page.**
MexHome already has `/agents/` and `/es/agents/`. No action needed in response to Diamante's agents page update.

**6. Monitor Diamante's bilingual agent profile strategy but do not replicate it reactively.**
MexHome has an established agent profile structure. Diamante's consistent bilingual agent page publishing is a standard practice, not a new strategic move. Ensure MexHome's agent profiles are complete, optimized, and internally linked from relevant area pages. No new builds are needed in response to the Emma Nicholson pages.
